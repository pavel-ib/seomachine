# TITAN STORY-VSL — The Native Video Format

> **This is not a traditional VSL.** The viewer must never feel like they're
> watching an ad. They think they're learning something. The product appears
> only after the story has earned it. This is the format that produced Fabula's
> best-performing creative in two years.

---

## THE CORE INSIGHT

Standard DR copy activates **persuasion knowledge** — the viewer's mental
filter that says "this is an ad, be skeptical." Everything that follows gets
evaluated through that filter. Claims get doubted. Benefits get minimized.

The Story-VSL bypasses this entirely. Open with a historical figure doing
something extraordinary and the brain registers it as **content** — the same
category as a documentary or podcast. Persuasion knowledge doesn't activate.
By the time the product appears, the viewer has already emotionally accepted
the entire premise the product is built on.

**The sale happens inside the story, not after it.**

---

## THE 5 INVARIABLE BONES

These never change regardless of length, product, or story angle:

```
1. HISTORICAL HOOK     — Person + one extraordinary, specific fact
2. PROOF               — Why we believe them (brief — earns the pivot)
3. PIVOT               — "But here's the part nobody talks about"
4. THE THING REMOVED   — What they had → how modern life stripped it
5. RESTORATION + CTA   — Product gives it back, risk-free
```

The STORY changes. The BONES don't.

## WHAT TITAN PSYCHOLOGY TO EMBED (AND WHAT TO SKIP)

The story format is the CONTAINER. Titan psychology is the FUEL inside.
Apply the psychological layer — NOT the structural DR layer.

### USE THESE (invisible to viewer, make the story hit harder)

**Neurochemical sequencing — engineer deliberately:**
```
Bone 1 (Hook)         = Cortisol spike (novelty + contrast)
Bone 2 (Proof)        = Dopamine (anticipation — "what's the secret?")
Bone 3 (Pivot)        = Dopamine surge (discovery moment)
Bone 4 (Removed)      = Cortisol + fractionation DOWN (loss, anger)
Bone 4 (Symptom)      = Cortisol sustained (fractionation DOWN)
Bone 5 (Restoration)  = Dopamine UP
Bone 5 (CTA)          = Hold UP through close
```

**Fractionation** — The format bounces DOWN/UP naturally. Make it deliberate.
History = UP. "Then it was removed" = DOWN. "That's why you feel X" = deeper
DOWN. "But now you can get it back" = UP. Engineer the depth of each swing.

**Blame transfer (Bias #24)** — Symptom hijack always ends with "it's not
you" beat. One sentence. Non-negotiable. "You're not unprepared. You were just never given the right system."

**Emotional specificity only** — Filter Titan's specificity principle:
- TIME specifics: "Tuesday" not "the week"
- MONEY specifics: "$80 a month" not "expensive"
- SENSORY specifics: "pressed beneath the saddle"
- NOT intellectual specifics: empire names, historical citations slow pacing

**Open loops (Zeigarnik)** — The pivot is one loop. Add a second inside
Bone 2: "And they did all of it on one fuel source" forces the viewer to
keep watching. Engineer two simultaneous loops that close at different points.

**Identity contrast** — Always punchy and time-specific. "Can't get through
Tuesday" beats "dragging through the week." Visceral over polite.

**Power words in pivot and enemy creation** — "Labeled waste." "Threw out."
"America believed it." Titan power words doing heavy lifting invisibly.

**Command close** — Last spoken line is always a command. Never a reflection.
"Don't wait." "Go get it." "Act on it." Non-negotiable at any length.

**UMP in the pivot** — Name the mechanism. "The Organization Gap." "The Freeze."
Every story-video should have one named mechanism that explains WHY
the historical system worked and WHY modern consumer kits lost it.

**One product differentiator connected to the story** — Inside Bone 5, one
specific detail that links backward to the story:
- Nightingale organized supplies by category → SurviveX uses color-coded compartments
- Battlefield medics carried organized trauma kits → SurviveX brings that system to families
This is Titan's concentration principle compressed to one line.

### SKIP THESE (designed for longer DR copy — hurt this format)

| Skip | Why |
|---|---|
| Credibility anchors longer than one phrase | Slow momentum, signal "this is an ad" |
| Named empire / intellectual specifics | Viewer processes, doesn't feel |
| Social proof sections | Kills native feel — save for landing page |
| Full crossroads close (3 paths) | No time — compress to identity contrast + command |
| Value stack breakdown | Landing page territory |
| Price anchors in enemy creation | "$80/month" is fine — detailed comparisons are not |
| Extended mechanism explanation | One named mechanism, one metaphor only |
| Long credibility anchors ("documented across multiple...") | One phrase max, say it fast |

## LENGTH VARIATIONS

### 3-MINUTE (~440 words @ 130 wpm)
All 5 bones fully developed. Full emotional journey.

```
Bone 1: Hook          20-30s    ~55 words
Bone 2: Proof         20-30s    ~55 words
Bone 3: Pivot         5-10s     ~15 words
Bone 4: Removed       60-80s    ~170 words (education + enemy + symptoms)
Bone 5: Restoration   40-50s    ~110 words (product + contrast + CTA)
Target: 430-460 words
```

### 1-MINUTE (~130 words @ 130 wpm)
Cut Bone 2 entirely. Compress Bone 4 to 3 sentences. Run Bones 1, 3, 4, 5.

```
Bone 1: Hook          10s       ~20 words (one fact only)
Bone 3: Pivot         5s        ~10 words
Bone 4: Removed       20s       ~40 words (enemy + symptoms compressed)
Bone 5: Restoration   25s       ~55 words (product + CTA)
Target: 125-135 words
```

**The rule:** Every section cut for the 1-minute version gets replaced by
one punchy sentence or removed entirely. No half-developed sections.

### MASTER → CUT WORKFLOW

Write the 3-minute first. Everything else is cut from it. Same shoot — multiple deliverables.

**Data insight:** 99.5% of winning Facebook/Instagram video ads are under 1 minute. The 3-minute master is for YouTube and landing page. Facebook needs the 45-60 second version as the PRIMARY creative.

| Cut | Length | Platform | How to make it |
|---|---|---|---|
| Master | 3-min (~440 words) | YouTube, landing page | Write first — all 5 bones |
| Facebook primary | 60-sec (~130 words) | Facebook feed | Remove Bone 2, compress Bone 4 to 3 sentences |
| Instagram primary | 45-sec (~95 words) | Instagram feed/Reels | Same as 60-sec, tighten Bone 5 to 2 sentences |
| Retargeting hook | 15-sec (~30 words) | All platforms, warm audiences | Hook B (direct) only + CTA |

**Making the 60-sec cut:**
1. Remove Bone 2 entirely
2. Compress Bone 4: enemy creation (2 sentences) + one symptom + "you're not broken"
3. Compress Bone 5: product (2 sentences) + identity contrast + CTA
Target: 125-135 words

**Making the 45-sec cut:**
Same as 60-sec, then compress Bone 5 to: product name + one differentiator + guarantee + command close.
Target: 90-100 words

## DUAL-HOOK OUTPUT (STANDARD — Always Deliver Both)

Every story-VSL deliverable includes TWO hook versions. The body (Bones 2-5) is identical. Only Bone 1 changes. Same shoot — re-record the first 15 seconds.

### HOOK A — Story Hook (cold traffic, persuasion bypass)
Opens with historical figure or "Did you know?" fact. Viewer enters learning mode.
~20-30 seconds. Viewer has no idea this is an ad until the product appears in Bone 5.

### HOOK B — Direct Hook (warm traffic, problem awareness)
Opens with avatar callout + specific symptom. Viewer self-selects immediately.
~15-20 seconds. Then bridges directly into Bone 3 (Pivot) — skips Bones 1 and 2.

```
HOOK B STRUCTURE:
Line 1: Avatar callout + time-specific symptom
         "If you can't get through Tuesday without hitting a wall..."
Line 2: Open loop / curiosity bridge into pivot
         "Here's something nobody's told you about why."
Line 3: [Pivot — same as Bone 3 in the story version]
         "But here's the part nobody talks about."
Then: Bone 4 + Bone 5 unchanged
```

### HOOK B FORMULAS (pick one per script)
- `"If you [symptom] every [time-specific]... here's something nobody's explaining."`
- `"Here's why [symptom] has nothing to do with [false cause]."`
- `"Most people trying to fix [symptom] are missing the one thing [historical people] never did without."`

### WHY THIS WORKS
- Story hook wins on cold audiences (no brand awareness, no problem awareness)
- Direct hook wins on warm audiences (problem-aware, scrolling for solutions)
- You find out which traffic temperature performs at which cost per result
- Cheapest split test possible — one shoot, two openings

### OUTPUT FORMAT
Always deliver in this order:
1. Hook A (story opening) — full 3-minute script
2. Hook B (direct opening) — first 15-20 seconds only, then note "Body continues from Bone 3 identical to Hook A"
3. 1-minute cut (uses Hook A by default — test Hook B version if Hook A wins)

## STORY SELECTION CRITERIA

A story qualifies only if it hits ALL THREE:

**1. Extraordinary performance** — Measurable, impressive, makes viewer lean
in. "A gallon of coffee a day." "Conquered 12 million square miles." NOT
"they were healthy" or "they lived long lives."

**2. Natural bridge to product** — The historical practice logically connects
to what you sell. Force the connection and it breaks. Viewer will feel it.

**3. Something was removed** — Modern life must have stripped away what they
had. This creates the enemy and the product's reason to exist. If the practice
is still common today, there is no story.

## CREATIVE SCALING TACTICS

**Multiple hooks, one story** — Script stays identical. Only the first
sentence changes. Test 3-4 hooks against the same story. Best hook wins,
everything else is already proven. Cheapest split test you'll run.

**Different emotional registers, same facts** — Same story, three versions:
- Triumph angle: "They won because of what they ate" (aspirational)
- Stolen angle: "They took this from you" (anger)
- Discovery angle: "Nobody talks about this" (curious)
Three distinct creatives from one story bank.

**A/B the identity contrast** — Same full script, different final contrast
line before CTA. Easy variable to test for conversion lift.

## THE INTEGRATED TEMPLATE (BONE × TITAN LAYER)

```
BONE 1: HISTORICAL HOOK
Titan: Cortisol spike | Curiosity gap (open loop 1) | Authority (historical figure)
Rule: One extraordinary fact. One person. Nothing else.

BONE 2: PROOF
Titan: Emotional specificity only | Open loop 2 planted | Fractionation UP
Rule: Named achievements the viewer can feel, not intellectual details.
      End with: "And they did all of it on one fuel source."

BONE 3: PIVOT
Titan: Zeigarnik pattern interrupt | Dopamine trigger | Close loop 1
Rule: "But here's the part nobody talks about." Always this beat.
      Then name WHAT was under the saddle/in the cup/behind the practice.

BONE 4: THE THING REMOVED
Titan: Named UMP | Blame transfer | Enemy creation | Emotional specifics
      | Fractionation DOWN | "It's not your fault" beat | Close loop 2
Rule: Education → Enemy creation → Symptom hijack → "You're not broken"
      Use $X/month, day-of-week specifics. No intellectual specifics.

BONE 5: RESTORATION + CTA
Titan: Product as UMS | One story-connected differentiator | Brief identity
      contrast (punchy, time-specific) | Supply-based scarcity | 90-day
      guarantee | Command close
Rule: Product appears. One differentiator. Identity contrast. Don't wait.
```

## QUALITY CHECK (BEFORE SHOOTING)

**Hook A (Story):**
- [ ] Does the first sentence contain a famous person/group + extraordinary fact?
- [ ] Does the viewer learn something real before the product appears?
- [ ] Is the pivot the exact phrase "but here's the part nobody talks about"?
- [ ] Is the UMP named (e.g., "The Organization Gap", "The Freeze")?
- [ ] Does the symptom hijack use time/money specifics, not generalities?
- [ ] Is there an "it's not you" beat before the product?
- [ ] Does the product connect backward to the historical story?
- [ ] Is the identity contrast time-specific (Tuesday, not "the week")?
- [ ] Is scarcity supply-based with a real reason?
- [ ] Does the last line end in a command ("Don't wait." "Act on it.")?
- [ ] Is the FDA disclaimer present at end?
- [ ] Word count: 1-min = 125-135 | 3-min = 430-460?

**Hook B (Direct — always deliver alongside Hook A):**
- [ ] Does line 1 call out the avatar with a time-specific symptom?
- [ ] Does line 2 bridge into the pivot without naming the product?
- [ ] Does Hook B connect cleanly into Bone 3 of the same body?
- [ ] Is Hook B 15-20 seconds max (~30-40 words)?
