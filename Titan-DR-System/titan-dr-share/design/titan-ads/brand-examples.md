## 1. SurviveX (50 ads, 90d avg winning)

**Category**: First aid kits / emergency preparedness / EMT-designed safety gear
**Visual Style**: Dark navy/tactical (#1A1F3D), safety orange accents, professional-but-approachable
**Winning Patterns Used**: Product Hero (#2), Testimonial Billboard (#6), Dark Mode (#15)

**Top Creative**: "YOUR FIRST AID KIT IS A BOX OF FALSE CONFIDENCE" — dark tactical background, open kit showing color-coded compartments
- Pattern: Product Hero + Bold Claim
- Why it works: Provocative headline stops scroll, kit interior centered, clean layout
- Trust badge at bottom: "Designed by EMT-Paramedic Chase Carter"

**Top Creative**: Delivery truck billboard — testimonial on moving truck
- Pattern: Testimonial Billboard
- Why it works: Implies scale, real customer quote, product visible
- Quote: "Had the wound closed in under 60 seconds. This kit is the real deal." — Mark T.

**Brand Rules**: Always includes EMT-curated credential. Dark tactical brand color. SurviveX wordmark at top.

## Cross-Brand Patterns

### What Winning Brands Have in Common
1. **Consistent visual identity** — same colors, fonts, layout across all ads
2. **One message per creative** — never try to say everything in one ad
3. **Product is always visible** (except problem-state ads)
4. **Social proof somewhere** — in image, body copy, or headline
5. **Clear CTA** — SHOP_NOW (66%) or LEARN_MORE (30%)

### What Differentiates #1 Performers
1. **Mechanism education** — SurviveX, MyMedic explain HOW their kits work differently
2. **Emotional scroll-stoppers** — SurviveX, AMK problem-state photos (real emergencies, disorganized kits)
3. **Story-format ads** — SurviveX Burn Kit, text-heavy cards that feel like content not ads
4. **UGC at scale** — SurviveX Large Pro, Rhino Rescue use creator networks and real customer demos
