# Titan Ads — Creative Patterns Library

> 15 visual layout patterns extracted from 1,255 winning DTC ads (GetHookd, March 2026).
> Each pattern includes: visual structure, when to use, copy placement, and real examples.

---

## PATTERN 1: Problem-State Photo

**Visual**: Close-up photo of person experiencing the problem. No product visible. Raw, emotional, real.
**Aspect**: 4:5 (Instagram/FB feed) or 9:16 (Stories/Reels)
**Copy on Image**: None or minimal (problem is the image)
**Copy in Ad Body**: Hook that names the feeling → mechanism → CTA
**Best For**: Top-of-funnel scroll-stopping. The "I feel seen" moment.

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Parent opening a messy first aid kit during a child's injury — panic visible (disorganization failure)
- SurviveX: Family on hiking trail, child holding bleeding knee, parent digging through a generic pouch (unprepared moment)
- SurviveX: Close-up of expired supplies and loose bandages with dashed circle annotation overlay

**Production Notes**:
- Gemini excels at photorealistic problem-state images
- Use 50mm lens, shallow DOF, natural light for authenticity
- Annotations (dashed circles, arrows) can be added via ImageMagick after generation
- NO text overlay — let the image do the emotional work
- Ad body copy carries the entire message

**Performance Signal**: SurviveX problem-state disorganized kit ads ran 70+ days winning

## PATTERN 2: Product Hero + Bold Claim

**Visual**: Product front-and-center, clean background (solid color or gradient), large bold headline text
**Aspect**: 4:5 or 1:1
**Copy on Image**: 1 bold headline (3-8 words max) + optional subhead
**Copy in Ad Body**: Supports the claim with mechanism/proof
**Best For**: Product awareness, brand recall, launching new products

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: "YOUR FIRST AID KIT IS A BOX OF FALSE CONFIDENCE" — dark tactical background, open kit
- SurviveX: "Don't let 'it won't happen to me' be your emergency plan" — single kit, safety orange tones
- SurviveX Large Pro: Dark mode, kit floating, safety orange accents

**Layout Blueprint**:
```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│     [BRAND LOGO]        │
│                         │
│   BOLD HEADLINE         │
│   (3-8 words)           │
│                         │
│     [PRODUCT IMAGE]     │
│     centered, large     │
│                         │
│   [subtext / disclaimer]│
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Production Notes**:
- Background: solid color (#F5F0EB cream, #1A1F3D tactical navy, #1A1A1A dark) or subtle gradient
- Product image: real photo or 3D render, with shadow for depth
- Headline font: Bold sans-serif (Impact, Montserrat Black, or brand font)
- Keep it to ONE message — don't dilute
- Disclaimer text at bottom in 8-10pt if making health claims

## PATTERN 3: UGC Selfie + Product

**Visual**: Real person (or realistic UGC-style) holding/showing the product, selfie angle, home environment
**Aspect**: 9:16 (Stories) or 4:5 (Feed)
**Copy on Image**: None — pure UGC aesthetic
**Copy in Ad Body**: First-person testimonial or "I tried this and..."
**Best For**: Mid-funnel trust building, retargeting, social proof

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Parent in kitchen opening kit and showing color-coded compartments (219d winning)
- Multiple safety/preparedness brands use influencer-style selfies and unboxing content

**Production Notes**:
- Cannot be generated by AI — requires real UGC or licensed stock
- If generating: Gemini can create UGC-style photos but they read as "stock" at close inspection
- Best approach: Use real UGC for the photo, add text overlay or ad body copy
- Authenticity markers: messy background, natural light, non-professional pose

## PATTERN 4: Illustrated / Cartoon Hook

**Visual**: Cartoon or illustration style, often depicting the problem in a slightly humorous/exaggerated way
**Aspect**: 1:1
**Copy on Image**: Bold text hook above or below the illustration
**Best For**: Pattern interrupt in feed, less "ad-like", FB policy friendly (no real human bodies)

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Cartoon family looking confused during a kitchen accident, text "Most families with a first aid kit are missing these 5 critical items, but did you know... more"

**Layout Blueprint**:
```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│ Bold text hook (black    │
│ on white, 3-4 lines)    │
│ ending with "...more"   │
├─────────────────────────┤
│                         │
│   [ILLUSTRATION]        │
│   depicting the problem │
│                         │
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Production Notes**:
- The "...more" truncation is intentional — creates curiosity gap
- Illustration style: flat color, thick outlines, comic-book adjacent
- Can be generated via Gemini with style prompts: "flat vector illustration, thick outlines, warm colors"
- Text is separate — overlay after generation

## PATTERN 5: Text-Heavy Card (Story/Letter Format)

**Visual**: Solid background color, emoji/icon at top, long-form text formatted as a letter or story
**Aspect**: 4:5
**Copy on Image**: THE ENTIRE AD — 150-300 words of story copy
**Copy in Ad Body**: Minimal (the image IS the copy)
**Best For**: Brand story, apology angle, early access, behind-the-scenes

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Dark navy background, warning emoji + first aid emoji, "WE MADE A MISTAKE" — full story about realizing most home kits lack burn treatment → created dedicated Burn Kit → free shipping on $75+

**Layout Blueprint**:
```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│      [EMOJI(S)]         │
│                         │
│   BOLD HEADLINE         │
│   (all caps, white)     │
│                         │
│   Story paragraph 1     │
│   (centered, lighter)   │
│                         │
│   Story paragraph 2     │
│   (centered, lighter)   │
│                         │
│   **OFFER LINE**        │
│   (bold, accent color)  │
│                         │
│   Urgency line          │
│                         │
│   Sign-off              │
│      -Brand Team 💜     │
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Production Notes**:
- This is a text-as-image ad — create in Figma, Canva, or HTML→screenshot
- NOT suitable for Gemini generation (text rendering unreliable)
- Background: solid dark (#1E1B4B navy, #1A1A1A black, #2D3748 slate)
- Text hierarchy: headline 28-32pt, body 16-18pt, offer 20-24pt bold
- Works because it looks like a social post, not an ad

## PATTERN 6: Testimonial Billboard

**Visual**: Product testimonial placed on a real-world surface (billboard, truck, bus stop, storefront)
**Aspect**: 4:5
**Copy on Image**: Testimonial quote on the "billboard" surface + product image
**Best For**: Social proof at scale, implies massive brand reach

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Delivery truck with tactical billboard showing testimonial quote + Large Kit product
  - Quote: "Had the wound closed in under 60 seconds. This kit is the real deal." — Mark T.

**Production Notes**:
- Gemini can generate photorealistic scenes with billboards/trucks
- Challenge: text on the billboard needs to be composited AFTER (Gemini text in images is unreliable)
- Workflow: Generate scene with blank billboard area → composite text + product mockup
- This pattern implies scale — "we're so big we're on trucks"

## PATTERN 7: Product + Review Quote

**Visual**: Product photo (usually studio shot) with customer review quote alongside
**Aspect**: 1:1
**Copy on Image**: 5-star rating + review quote (60-100 words) + offer badge
**Best For**: Trust building, mid-funnel, retargeting visitors who didn't convert

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Large Kit product shot + review quote on light gray background
  - "Honestly didn't expect much from another first aid kit but this system is legit..."
  - "FREE SHIPPING ON $75+" badge + "SHOP NOW" button

**Layout Blueprint**:
```
┌─────────────────────────────────┐
│          ★★★★★                  │
│                                 │
│ "Review quote text here,        │
│  2-4 sentences that tell a      │
│  mini transformation story."    │
│           — Name                │
│                                 │
│  [PRODUCT]    ┌──────────┐      │
│  (left)       │ 70% OFF  │      │
│               │TODAY ONLY│      │
│               ├──────────┤      │
│               │ SHOP NOW │      │
│               └──────────┘      │
└─────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Production Notes**:
- Use real product photos (from brand assets or Figma extraction)
- Quote selection: choose reviews that mention specific results + timeframe
- Offer badge: high contrast (orange on gray, red on white, yellow on dark)
- Can be composited: product photo + text overlay + badge

## PATTERN 8: Holiday / Event Offer

**Visual**: Product photo with seasonal/holiday themed background or accents
**Aspect**: 1:1
**Copy on Image**: Event name + offer + "BUY X GET Y" or "X% OFF"
**Best For**: Seasonal campaigns, urgency, clear offers

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: American flag background, first aid kit products, "MEMORIAL OFFER — FREE SHIPPING ALL WEEKEND" + "PROTECT YOUR FAMILY"
- SurviveX Waterproof: Outdoor adventure lifestyle shot, "Summer Sale — Large Waterproof Kit + Free Shipping!"

**Layout Blueprint**:
```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│  EVENT HEADLINE          │
│  (bold, 2 lines max)    │
│                         │
│     [PRODUCT IMAGE]     │
│  (with seasonal accents)│
│                         │
├─────────────────────────┤
│  OFFER LINE             │
│  (high contrast bar)    │
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Production Notes**:
- Holiday accents: flag elements, gift bows, snowflakes, hearts (Valentine's)
- Top bar + bottom bar framing is common
- Product should still be the hero — seasonal elements are supporting
- Red bar with white text is the most common offer strip

## PATTERN 9: Us vs Them / Comparison

**Visual**: Split layout or comparison showing YOUR product vs competitors (or old way vs new way)
**Aspect**: 4:5 or 1:1
**Copy on Image**: Comparison data points, price callouts, ingredient callouts
**Best For**: Differentiation, conquesting, competitive positioning

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: "YOUR FIRST AID KIT IS A BOX OF FALSE CONFIDENCE" — dark tactical background, open kit showing compartments
  - Callout bubbles: "Generic Kits = No Organization, No Labels", "Most Brands Dump 50 Supplies in One Pouch", "That's Why You Freeze When It Matters"
  - Bottom: "COLOR-CODED. ORGANIZED. LIFE-SAVING. SURVIVEX."

**Production Notes**:
- Split compositions work well: left (competitor/old way) vs right (your product)
- Comparison callouts with icon bubbles add credibility
- Dark backgrounds with gold/warm accents = premium positioning
- Aggressive copy works here: directly calling out competitor weaknesses
- Gemini can generate the product/scene; callouts composited after

## PATTERN 10: Urgency / Scarcity Card

**Visual**: Dark background, product image, sale messaging with countdown/finality language
**Aspect**: 1:1
**Copy on Image**: "WE'RE SAYING GOODBYE" / "LAST CHANCE" / "ENDS TONIGHT" + offer details
**Best For**: Bottom-of-funnel, cart abandoners, end-of-sale pushes

**Examples**:
- SurviveX Zip Stitch: Black background, "LIMITED BATCH — BACK IN STOCK" in huge white text, product image, FREE SHIPPING

**Production Notes**:
- Emotional language of loss/ending outperforms countdown timers
- Black/dark backgrounds create visual weight and seriousness
- Product small, text large — the urgency IS the creative
- Works as text-on-solid overlay — no Gemini needed

## PATTERN 11: Before / After Split

**Visual**: Side-by-side or stacked comparison of before and after states
**Aspect**: 1:1 or 4:5
**Copy on Image**: "BEFORE" / "AFTER" labels, timeframe
**Best For**: Transformation proof, hair/skin/weight products
**Compliance Note**: Meta restricts B/A for weight loss. Hair/skin is generally safe. No misleading edits.

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Disorganized generic kit vs organized SurviveX color-coded kit comparison

**Production Notes**:
- Use real customer photos when possible (UGC)
- Same lighting, same angle for credibility
- Include timeframe: "After 8 weeks" builds believability
- For supplements: focus on energy, appearance, mood — not disease claims

## PATTERN 12: Mechanism Explainer

**Visual**: Educational layout showing HOW the product works, with callouts and visual elements
**Aspect**: 4:5 or 9:16
**Copy on Image**: 3-5 callout points with icons explaining the mechanism
**Best For**: Education, TOF for complex products, building "aha" moments

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Open kit interior with icon callout bubbles explaining color-coded compartment system
  - Each callout has small icon + 1-line explanation (Wounds=red, Antiseptic=blue, Hygiene=teal, Tools=black)

**Production Notes**:
- Icon + text callout bubbles are the key element
- Central product/ingredient image with callouts radiating out
- Background: gradient (dark→warm) for premium feel
- This pattern educates AND sells simultaneously
- Works for: ingredients, process, science-backed claims

## PATTERN 13: Ingredient / Science Visual

**Visual**: Close-up of ingredients, raw materials, or scientific imagery
**Aspect**: 1:1 or 4:5
**Copy on Image**: Ingredient names, "clinically studied" badges
**Best For**: Authority building, health-conscious audiences

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: Close-up of Zip Stitch wound closure strips, medical-grade components
- Various: Hemostatic gauze close-ups, Israeli bandage detail shots, burn gel macro shots

**Production Notes**:
- Macro photography style: tight crop, soft background, ingredient in sharp focus
- Gemini generates excellent food/ingredient macro shots
- Pair with copy that names specific compounds, dosages
- "Clinically studied" / "3rd party tested" badges add authority

## PATTERN 14: Listicle / Reasons Why

**Visual**: Numbered list or checklist format with benefits
**Aspect**: 4:5
**Copy on Image**: 3-7 bullet points or numbered reasons
**Best For**: Multi-benefit messaging, overcoming objections

**Examples**:
- SurviveX: iOS Notes-app style checklist of kit features
- Various: "5 Reasons Families Are Upgrading Their First Aid Kit..." format

**Layout Blueprint**:
```
┌─────────────────────────┐
│  HEADLINE               │
│  "X Reasons [audience]  │
│   Are Trying [product]" │
│                         │
│  ✅ Reason 1            │
│  ✅ Reason 2            │
│  ✅ Reason 3            │
│  ✅ Reason 4            │
│  ✅ Reason 5            │
│                         │
│  [PRODUCT] or [CTA]     │
└─────────────────────────┘
```

**Production Notes**:
- iOS Notes style is trending — fake screenshot aesthetic
- Green checkmarks or numbered circles for each point
- Keep each point to 1 line (6-10 words)
- Can be created as text overlay on solid background — no Gemini needed

## PATTERN 15: Dark Mode / Premium

**Visual**: Black or very dark background, product with dramatic lighting, minimal text
**Aspect**: 4:5 or 1:1
**Copy on Image**: Product name + 1 short tagline
**Best For**: Luxury positioning, premium supplements, men's products

**Examples**:
- SurviveX Large Pro: Dark navy/black, floating kit, dramatic rim lighting
- SurviveX Zip Stitch: Black packaging on dark background with safety orange accent

**Production Notes**:
- Gemini excels at dark-mode product shots with dramatic lighting
- JSON prompt: high contrast, rim lighting, dark background, product floating
- Minimal text — let the aesthetic do the positioning
- Works for: men's supplements, premium lines, "clinical" products
