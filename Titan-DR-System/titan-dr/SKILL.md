| Format | Pricing/Offer Details | Product Name Timing | Tone |
|--------|----------------------|---------------------|------|
| **Pre-sale / Listicle** | NONE. No prices, no discounts, no offer terms. Curiosity only. | Minimal — focus on problem/solution, not product | Educational, discovery |
| **Advertorial** | NONE in body. Soft mention at end only. | Delayed until 50-70% through copy | Editorial, journalistic |
| **Landing / Sales Page** | FULL — pricing, bundles, urgency, offer stack | Throughout — this IS the product page | Direct, conversion-focused |
| **VSL (Roosevelt format)** | Delayed until close section | After story bridge (word 250+) | Story-led, emotional |
| **Facebook Ad** | Teaser only (e.g., "up to X% off") | Can appear early — short format | Scroll-stopping, punchy |

## Execution Workflow

### Step 1: Identify Format and Avatar
What is the deliverable format? Who is the target avatar?

### Step 2: Load Files (Smart Routing — see table above)
Load 3 core files + 1-2 format files using Read tool. Confirm all loaded.

### Step 3: Strategic Assessment (from titan-psychology.md) — MANDATORY OUTPUT
Determine and explicitly state ALL of:
- **Awareness Level** (1-5) → headline type, lead structure, proof intensity
- **Sophistication Stage** (1-5) → mechanism emphasis vs. claim vs. identity
- **Named Mechanism** — REQUIRED: Name the UMP (Unique Mechanism of Problem) and UMS (Unique Mechanism of Solution). Every piece of copy must have a named mechanism beyond just the product name. E.g., "Chaos Kit Syndrome" (UMP) → "Organization Under Pressure" (UMS).
- **Dominant emotional driver** → cortisol/dopamine/oxytocin sequence for this avatar
- **Voice Register** (R1-R20) from the 20-register system in titan-language.md
- **Biases to deploy** — list 8-12 by name for this specific piece

### Step 4: Draft Using Format Structure
Use the format file's structural blueprint exactly — sequence, block types, what belongs where.

**Word Count:** If the brief specifies a word count or duration, plan section-by-section word budgets BEFORE writing. Count words and validate against target before finalizing.

**Product Name Placement Rules:**
- **VSL (Roosevelt format / story-led):** Product/substance name appears ONLY after story bridge — typically word 250+. First third = problem + story only.
- **Pre-sale / Listicle:** NO pricing details, NO offer specifics. Curiosity only. Product name minimal.
- **Advertorial:** Product name delayed until 50-70% through copy. Editorial tone throughout.
- **Landing page / Sales page:** Full pricing, offer stack, urgency — product name throughout.
- **Facebook ad:** Product can appear early — short format demands it.

### Step 5: Apply Language Engine (from titan-language.md)
- Headline formula based on awareness level
- Schwartz Strengtheners on every headline and key claim
- NLP patterns embedded throughout body copy
- Metaphor from swipe file for mechanism explanation
- Eliminate all 10 Conversion Killers
- Quality Gate: 10 lenses, 90+ threshold

### Step 6: Compliance Check (from titan-words-forbidden.md)
- Zero Tier 1 triggers anywhere — INCLUDING inside testimonials and social proof
- All Tier 2 terms shielded (correct Shield Protocol for each)
- No "YOU + Negative" framing (Personal Attributes rule)
- Testimonial disclaimers present
- No disease names used as product benefit claims (use Study Citation Shield or Founder Story Defense)
- Run War Room audit: extract all health terms → check each against Tier system

### Step 7: Quality Output
Deliver copy. State: Awareness level used, Sophistication stage, Named mechanism (UMP/UMS), Register used, Format loaded, biases deployed (list by name and number), word count.

## The 7 Critical Rules (Apply to All Formats)

1. **Every sentence does at least two jobs.** Informs + persuades. Agitates + opens loop. Proves + builds desire.
2. **Benefits lead. Features justify.** Never reverse.
3. **"You" not "We."** The copy is about them.
4. **Short sentences. Fragments for emphasis. Like this.**
5. **Specific over general.** "$1.63/day" not "affordable." "31,482 people" not "thousands."
6. **Voice must match avatar exactly.** If it could be for anyone, it's for no one.
7. **Compliance always.** No Tier 1 trigger is ever worth the risk.
