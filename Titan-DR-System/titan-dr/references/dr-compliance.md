# TITAN DIRECT RESPONSE — COMPLIANCE GUARDRAILS (TITAN SHIELD)

Two separate risk systems govern first aid kit / medical device advertising. Confuse them and you either lose your account or leave money on the table.

**FDA RISK** = First aid kits containing medical devices (wound closures, bandages) fall under FDA regulation. Marketing claims must not position consumer products as medical treatments. Could trigger enforcement action.
**META BAN RISK** = Automated bot flags. Ad rejected in minutes. Account disabled. No traffic TODAY.

**We avoid Tier 1 (instant bans). We embrace Tier 2 (approved positioning with shields). This is where the money is made.**

### 4A. TIER 1: DEATH ZONE — NEVER USE

These trigger Meta's automated systems. Ad rejected in minutes. Repeated violations = account disabled.

| Trigger | Why It Kills |
|---------|-------------|
| "Saves lives" (as a direct product claim) | Medical outcome claim — instant flag |
| Before/After injury photos (graphic wounds) | Shocking content / medical imagery violation |
| "YOU + Negative Attribute" | Personal Attributes Policy violation |
| "Cures" / "Treats" / "Heals" (as product claims) | Medical treatment claim — instant flag |
| Drug/pharmaceutical comparisons | Medical category trigger |
| Claims about specific diseases or medical conditions | Serious health category |
| Fake play buttons / UI elements | Deceptive UI violation |
| "Guaranteed to stop bleeding" / "Prevents infection" | Unsubstantiated medical outcome claim |
| Graphic injury imagery in ad creative | Shocking/sensational content violation |

**THE PERSONAL ATTRIBUTES TRAP (Most Common Rejection):**
- BANNED: "Are you worried about your family getting hurt?" / "Are you unprepared?" / "Do you panic in emergencies?"
- APPROVED: "Many families discover their first aid kit isn't organized for real emergencies..."
- **Rule:** Shift from second-person accusation to third-person observation. Never assert the viewer IS or FEELS a condition. Describe it as something that HAPPENS to people.

### 4B. TIER 2: PROFIT ZONE — USE WITH SHIELDS

These terms carry some regulatory consideration but are NOT Meta bot triggers. Competitors use them on live pages across the first aid and preparedness category.

**APPROVED SITUATIONAL TERMS:**
Emergency preparedness, wound care, first aid response, trauma kit, field treatment, injury management, emergency readiness, tactical medical, wilderness first aid, household safety

**APPROVED BENEFIT VERBS:**
Organizes, Prepares, Equips, Enables, Supports, Provides, Contains, Includes, Features, Designed for, Helps you respond

**APPROVED OUTCOME FRAMING:**
"Be prepared," "Respond with confidence," "Know where everything is," "Act faster in emergencies," "Feel more confident," "Reduce response time," "Have what you need"

### 4C. THE 6 SHIELD PROTOCOLS (Required for ALL Tier 2 Usage)

**SHIELD 1: The Asterisk Shield**
Place asterisk (*) on outcome claims. Link to footer disclaimer. "Respond faster in emergencies*" — asterisk links to medical disclaimer.

**SHIELD 2: The Medical Disclaimer (Use This Exact Copy)**
"*SurviveX first aid kits are designed to help you respond to common injuries and emergencies. They are not a substitute for professional medical care. Always call 911 or seek professional medical attention for serious injuries or medical emergencies. Individual results and experiences may vary."

**SHIELD 3: The Testimonial Disclaimer**
"Individual experiences may vary. Testimonials reflect personal experiences and are not intended to represent or guarantee that anyone will achieve the same or similar results. Not a substitute for professional medical care."

**SHIELD 4: The Testimonial Loophole**
Put outcome claims INSIDE testimonials attributed to real, named customers. This shifts liability from brand claim to user story.
- Example: "I used the Zip Stitch on my son's chin at the lake. Avoided a 45-minute drive to the ER and $800 in bills. — Ryan T., Flagstaff, AZ"
- Deploy: Named customer + specific situation + personal experience language

**SHIELD 5: The Authority Citation Shield**
Professional endorsements appear in the context of expert opinion — NOT as direct product medical claims.
- "Chase Carter, EMT-P, designed the SurviveX organization system based on how paramedics organize trauma bags"
- Frame as: "The expert says..." or "Designed by..." not "This product will..."

**SHIELD 6: The Founder/Expert Story Defense**
Aggressive situational descriptions appear as CONTEXT in personal narrative, not product benefit claims.
- "After years of responding to emergencies and watching people freeze because they couldn't find supplies in their kits, I designed a system that eliminates the chaos."
- These are professional observations, not product outcome promises.

### 4D. COMPLIANCE QUICK-CHECK

Before publishing ANY first aid / emergency preparedness copy, verify:
- [ ] No Tier 1 triggers present anywhere (headline, body, image, landing page)
- [ ] All Tier 2 terms have at least one Shield active
- [ ] No "YOU + Negative" framing (use third-person observation)
- [ ] Testimonials have names, disclaimer, and "experiences may vary"
- [ ] No claims that the kit replaces professional medical care
- [ ] Medical disclaimer present in footer
- [ ] No graphic injury imagery (use clean product shots, organized kit photos)
- [ ] No direct "saves lives" claims attributed to the product (can appear in testimonials with shields)
- [ ] "Not a substitute for professional medical care" disclaimer present

### 4D-2. COMPLIANCE SCOPE: ADS vs. ON-PAGE COPY

Titan Shield tiers were built primarily for META ADS — what Meta's automated bot flags and rejects in minutes. On-page copy (landing pages, listicles, advertorials) operates under different enforcement.

**The distinction:**
- **Meta Ads:** Bot-scanned in real time. Tier 1 = instant rejection. Tier 2 = runs with shields. This is the strict zone.
- **On-Page Copy:** NOT scanned by Meta's ad review bot the same way. The landing page your ad links to has more freedom than the ad creative itself. FDA/FTC rules still apply, but Meta's automated enforcement is focused on the ad unit.

**What competitors do on-page (observed on live first aid / preparedness pages):**
- "Life-saving first aid kit" — used in on-page headlines (MyMedic)
- "Stop the bleed" — used in on-page educational content (NAR, Stop the Bleed campaign)
- "Treat injuries in the field" — used in on-page benefit copy (Uncharted Supply)
- "This kit saved my life" — used in on-page testimonial context (various)

**OUR APPROACH — Conservative On-Page:**
Even though competitors use aggressive terms on-page, we maintain shields on landing pages and listicles. Why:
1. FDA regulates medical devices (wound closure strips, bandages) regardless of Meta's enforcement
2. FTC monitors health and safety product advertising across all channels
3. Shield protocols (asterisks, disclaimers, testimonial attribution, authority citations) cost nothing to implement and protect against regulatory risk
4. Building compliant habits prevents accidental Tier 1 language from leaking into ads

**The Rule:**
- Write AD CREATIVE to full Shield spec (Tier 1/2 rules, strict)
- Write ON-PAGE COPY with more linguistic freedom BUT always with:
  - Medical disclaimer footer (Shield 2)
  - Asterisks on outcome claims (Shield 1)
  - Aggressive claims inside named testimonials (Shield 4)
  - Professional credentials cited for authority claims (Shield 5)
  - Testimonial disclaimer present (Shield 3)
  - "Not a substitute for professional medical care" present

**Bottom line:** More freedom on-page ≠ no shields. Competitors who skip shields are taking on risk we choose not to take. The shields are invisible to the reader and cost nothing to implement.

### 4E. META TECHNICAL SURVIVAL (2025+)

**The Health & Wellness Restriction:** As of January 2025, Meta restricts health/wellness brands from optimizing for "Purchase" and "Add to Cart" events. First aid kits may be classified in this category. 30-40% drop in ad efficiency reported.

**Workarounds:**
1. **Server-Side CAPI** — Send conversion data server-to-server, bypassing browser restrictions. Sanitize all health-related parameters before sending.
2. **Custom Events** — Replace "Purchase" with neutral names ("Complete_Order") via server-side implementation. NEVER rename events in browser — domain gets flagged.
3. **First-Party Data** — Collect emails/phone at checkout, upload to Meta for matching and lookalike building.
4. **Upper-Funnel Optimization** — If lower-funnel events blocked, optimize for Landing Page Views, Content Views, or Meta Instant Forms.
5. **Category Classification** — First aid kits may be classifiable under "Safety Equipment" or "Outdoor Gear" rather than "Health & Wellness." Test different product categorizations.

**IMMEDIATE ACTION:** Check domain classification in Meta Events Manager → Settings → Manage Data Source Categories. If incorrectly classified as "Health and Wellness," submit review request. First aid kits as "Safety/Emergency Equipment" may avoid health-category restrictions.
