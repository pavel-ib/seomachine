# TITAN QUIZ FUNNEL — The Complete Quiz Architecture

> **A quiz funnel is not a survey.** It's a psychological progression system.
> Every question is both a data collection point AND a persuasion mechanism.
> By the result page, the prospect has said yes 4-6 times and prescribed THEMSELVES
> the product — you just delivered the prescription they asked for.

---

## PART 1: THE THREE PSYCHOLOGICAL JOBS

A quiz funnel must do all three jobs. Missing any one collapses the conversion.

**Job 1: Pre-Qualification**
The quiz self-selects buyers who are problem-aware and ready to act. By answering 4-6 targeted questions about their specific situation, they've already acknowledged the problem EXISTS and it applies to THEM. Cold traffic becomes warm mid-funnel — without any selling.

**Job 2: Micro-Commitment Building (Bias #26 — Quiz Commitment)**
Each question = a small yes. Each small yes makes the next yes easier (Commitment/Consistency Bias #8). By the result page, prospect has compounding cognitive momentum toward purchase. Saying no at the result page now creates cognitive dissonance — they've already invested effort into YES.

**Job 3: Personalization Illusion**
Results are personalized to quiz answers. Functionally, all paths converge on the product — but the result page feels bespoke. "Based on YOUR lifestyle, you need X" converts dramatically harder than "Buy X." The product feels prescribed, not purchased.

## PART 2: OPTIMAL QUESTION ARCHITECTURE (5-Step Sequence)

| Q # | Type | Purpose | Example |
|-----|------|---------|---------|
| 1 | Identity/Goal | Easy entry, no pressure, motivation reveal | "What's your #1 reason for wanting a better first aid kit?" [Family safety / Outdoor activities / Travel / Workplace] |
| 2 | Current State | Pain validation — forces them to ASSESS their situation | "How prepared do you feel for a household emergency right now?" [Very prepared / Somewhat / Not really / Not at all] |
| 3 | Desire Activation (image-based) | Self-generated aspiration gap | "Choose the image that represents how you want to feel during an emergency" [Images: Calm & organized / Confident / In control] |
| 4 | Commitment Sizing | Establishes household needs + product recommendation | "How many people live in your household?" [1-2 / 3-4 / 5+ / Include pets] |
| 5 | Email Capture | After investment → abandonment drops sharply | "Where should we send your personalized preparedness plan?" |

**Q6+ (optional):** Additional lifestyle/health questions for OTO targeting and email segmentation. Should feel like genuine diagnostic questions — not visible as data collection.

## PART 3: THE IMAGE-BASED ASPIRATION GAP (Visual Selection Model)

**The most powerful desire-activation move in a quiz funnel:**

- **Q1:** "Choose your current body type" → image options (Slim / Average / Heavy)
- **Q2:** "Choose your desired body type" → image options (Fit / Athletic / Lean)

When users SEE and CHOOSE their desired state before any pitch, the aspiration-gap is **self-generated**. They created the gap. You didn't push it. This is stronger than any copy you could write — because they chose it themselves.

**Why it converts so well:** The gap between the two chosen images lives inside their head for the rest of the funnel. Every subsequent section reinforces a gap they built themselves.

**Apply this principle beyond body type:** Any visual selection of "current state vs. desired state" creates the same self-generated tension:
- First aid kit organization images (jumbled bag vs. color-coded system)
- Emergency response visuals (panicked vs. calm and in-control)
- Preparedness level illustrations (bare minimum vs. fully equipped)
- Family safety representation (text with emoji if images not available)

## PART 4: EMAIL CAPTURE TIMING

**Rule: ALWAYS after minimum Q3-4. Never before Q3.**

After Q1-2: Prospect is curious but hasn't invested. Abandonment at email capture is high.
After Q3-4: Prospect has clicked through 3-4 choices. The Zeigarnik Effect and IKEA Effect are both active — they NEED the result. Abandonment drops sharply.

**Email capture copy (never "Enter your email"):**
```
"Where should we send your personalized emergency preparedness plan?"
```
"Personalized preparedness plan" = 3x higher perceived value than "results." They're getting a custom recommendation — not a marketing email.

## PART 5: THE SCORING SYSTEM (Diagnostic Score Model)

A diagnostic score transforms a purchase into a prescription.

**The Scoring Range Rule:**
Always score in the **40-68 range** for the "problem exists" placement:
- Below 40: feels hopeless → resistance and skepticism
- Above 68: feels fine → no urgency, why buy?
- **40-68: urgent but improveable → optimal conversion window**

**Score Interpretation Copy Template:**
> "A score of [42] indicates your household emergency preparedness has significant gaps but is highly improveable with the right system. The good news: at this level, most families feel dramatically more confident within days of setting up the right kit."

**Score-to-Product Bridge:**
> "Based on your household size, activity level, and preparedness score, the kit that matches your profile is [Product Name]."

NOT: "Buy our product." 
YES: "Your profile matches this protocol."

The difference: one is a purchase, the other is a clinical recommendation.

## PART 6: THE RESULT PAGE FORMULA (6 Blocks)

**Block 1 — Personalized Headline:**
> "Based on your answers, here's the truth about your household's emergency preparedness..."

**Block 2 — Score Reveal + Interpretation:**
[Score display] + [severity reading] + [what it means specifically for them]
> "Your Preparedness Score: 42/100 — Your household has significant gaps but they're highly fixable."

**Block 3 — Recommendation Matched to Their Answers:**
Reference their specific quiz responses explicitly:
> "Because you indicated your household has 3-4 people, you're active outdoors, and you don't feel prepared for emergencies, your primary gap is likely organization and wound closure capability..."

**Block 4 — Product as Expert Recommendation:**
> "The kit designed for your exact household profile is [PRODUCT]. Here's why it addresses your specific gaps..."

**Block 5 — Social Proof (Profile-Matched):**
> "Families with your Preparedness Score who set up the SurviveX system reported [specific outcomes: feeling more confident, handling emergencies without ER visits, etc.]..."

**Block 6 — CTA:**
"Start your personalized [X] plan" + pricing (subscription first) + guarantee

---

## PART 7: RESULT PAGE TONE RULES

**DO:**
- Write as if delivering a personalized diagnosis
- Mirror their specific quiz answers back in the copy
- Use "your profile" and "based on your responses" throughout
- Make the product feel like the prescription, not the purchase
- Present subscription pricing first (make it the default)

**NEVER:**
- Use typical ad/sales language on the result page — breaks the diagnostic frame
- Show pricing before the mechanism explanation
- Use countdown clocks or fake urgency — clashes with "personalized result" positioning
- Use "you should buy" language — always "your protocol is" or "based on your profile"

| Element | Quiz Funnel | Never Import From Other Formats |
|---------|------------|--------------------------------|
| Questions 1-6 | Staged pain-aspiration reveals | Sales page blocks, comparison tables |
| Result page | Scored diagnostic, personalized | Landing page hero, pricing tiers, countdown timers |
| Email capture | After Q3-4 investment point | Before Q1 (kills abandonment metric) |
| CTA language | "Start your [X] plan" | "Buy now" / "Add to cart" / "Order today" |
| Social proof | Profile-matched ("people with your score...") | Generic testimonials, star ratings |
| Urgency | "Your score indicates the sooner you start..." | Countdown timers, stock counters |

## PART 9: QUIZ OTO ARCHITECTURE — The Post-Purchase Quiz Model (SurviveX)

**The concept:** After the main purchase, ask 2-3 more questions. The answers trigger a hyper-relevant upsell based on what they just revealed.

**How it works:**
1. Customer purchases main product
2. On the thank you page: "Before your order is complete, one quick question..."
3. Their answer reveals a specific additional need
4. OTO presented as the logical addition for their specific situation

**Why it dramatically outperforms standard OTOs:**
Standard OTO: "You might also like this." (generic → easy to ignore)
Quiz OTO: "Because you told us [X]..." (personalized → feels like it was made for them)

Conversion rates on answer-triggered OTOs far exceed standard "you might also like" OTOs.

## PART 10: COMPLIANCE IN QUIZ FUNNELS

Quiz funnels have specific compliance risks:

**Score system:** The score MUST NOT imply a medical diagnosis. Language: "lifestyle and wellness score" NOT "health diagnostic score." The score measures self-reported symptoms, not medical status.

**Personalized recommendation:** Must NOT say "you have [disease]" or "based on your [diagnosis]." Use: "Based on your responses" — not "based on your condition."

**Result page claims:** All health claims still require Shield 1 (asterisks) and Shield 2 (FDA disclaimer). Personalization doesn't exempt you from FDA compliance.

**Email capture:** CAN-SPAM and GDPR compliant opt-in language required. "By submitting, you consent to receive marketing communications from [Brand]."
